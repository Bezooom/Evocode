# Edit Article

Edit and improve articles by restructuring sections, improving clarity, and tightening prose.

## Process

### 1. Divide the article into sections

Based on its headings. Think about the main points you want to make during those sections.

Information is a directed acyclic graph — pieces of information depend on other pieces. Make sure that the order of the sections and their contents respects these dependencies.

Confirm the sections with the user.

### 2. For each section:

#### 2a. Rewrite the section

- Improve clarity, coherence, and flow
- Use maximum 240 characters per paragraph
- Cut unnecessary words
- Strengthen weak sentences
- Remove redundancy
- Keep the author's voice

#### 2b. Check for:

- **Logic flow**: Does each paragraph lead naturally to the next?
- **Evidence**: Are claims supported?
- **Structure**: Are there too many ideas in one paragraph? Too little?
- **Tone**: Consistent throughout?
- **Voice**: Active vs. passive, formal vs. casual?
- **Hooks**: Opening and closing are compelling?
- **Repetition**: Same word used too often?

### 3. Present the edited version

Show the user:
- The full edited article
- A summary of major changes
- Any sections where you made significant structural changes

### 4. Iterate

Ask the user:
- "Does this capture your voice?"
- "Any sections you'd like to see revised?"
- "Any additions or omissions?"
